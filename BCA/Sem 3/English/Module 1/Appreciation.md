"World Malayali" is a beautiful modernist poem by the Malayalam poet K. Ayyappa Paniker. It explores the themes of identity, roots , ancestry and belonging in a simple yet deep way.

The poem follows a curios "white boy" who searches for his true origin. He travels backwards through generations meeting [[World Malayali#Stanza 1|genes]], [[World Malayali#Stanza 2|sperms]], [[World Malayali#Stanza 3|chromosomes]],[[World Malayali#Stanza 5|flowers]],singers, and a rainbow. Each stops and gives answer that show identity is mixed and comes from many lands. Finally he reaches a simple Malayalam village scene by a canal with a fisherman and housewife. Here, through a symbolic blush truing into a lotus and a bee spreading pollen, he realizes his root are in Malayalam soil.
He returns home as a proud "World Malayali" someone rooted in Kerala but connected to the whole world.
This poem is very relevant today, especially for Malayali living around the world.
It gives a comforting message that your identity can be both deeply local and global at the same time.
